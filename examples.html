Examples

You will need stereo headphones to gain a better understanding of the observed phenomena.

The color markers in the image represent the part of the image that generated the echo sound that is being played at that moment.

The bottom graph in the image represents the amplitudes of the signal, as they change through time.

The beep with a different frequency at the beginning of each image signals the time when the virtual speaker in the camera would emit a sound.

The sound in the animations below is travelling very slowly so that the differences become apparent with no effort. In practice the slowdown does not have to be so large to make the differences observable.

The images in the animations below were created by aligning a video frame of the camera with a depth frame. As the field of view of the RGB sensor produving the video frames is smaller than the field of view of the depth sensor, there is a black area around the colored image that still produces echos. Some parts of the color images also have unusual black lines. These lines are an artifact of aligning video frames with depth frames and correspond to regions where the depth frame had no depth information. The depth vision camera is a stereo camera, and therefore close to sharp edges or corners the depth can not be accurately determined.

In all clips the camera is as if it were attached to a chest-mount camera holder, facing slightly downwards from the horizontal level.
